There are ten scenarios where you have to decide between two lotteries (A and B). The payoffs are identical across all scenarios, but the probabilities vary.

You begin with an endowment of {{ C.HLLFinitial }} p.

You stand to lose either a large or a small portion of your endowment depending on whether you win a lottery.

The probability of winning a lottery is given in the row. Note that regardless of which lottery you choose in each row, the probability of winning is identical for both lotteries in the same row. For each scenario, you must select which lottery you want to play.

Lottery A
You will only lose {{ C.A_win_loss }} p if you win the lottery.
However you will lose {{ C.A_lose_loss }} p if you lose the lottery.

Lottery B
You will only lose {{ C.B_win_loss }} p if you win the lottery.
However you will lose {{ C.B_lose_loss }} p if you lose the lottery.
